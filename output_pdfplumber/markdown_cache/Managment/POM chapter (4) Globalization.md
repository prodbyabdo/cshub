### Page Content (Page 16)

> Management styles
> 1. Authoritative
> An authoritative manager follows a top-down approach to
> leading.
> Pros: Useful when it’s necessary to make effective
> decisions quickly.
> Cons: New and innovative ideas rarely emerge
> 2. Consultative
> They often have an open-door policy that encourages
> employees to share what is and isn’t working in the
> organization.
> Pros: Often leads to higher employee engagement.
> Cons: A consultative management style isn’t always
> efficient as many people are involved in making decisions.

---

### Page Content (Page 17)
**Category**: Raw Text

> Management styles
> 3. Democratic or participative
> This style includes effective communication and openness through all
> levels of the organization.
> Pros: Encourages them to tap into their full potential at work.
> Cons: Consumes time as Decision-making often involves debates and
> consulting multiple parties.
> 4. Laissez-faire
> In this management style, managers monitor what’s happening with
> employees, but don’t become too involved with the day-to-day tasks.
> Pros: It gives self-motivated employees the space they need to be
> productive. This could be particularly useful in a creative
> environment.
> Cons: Because this management style is hands-off, it can leave some
> employees feeling neglected or in need of guidance and direction.

---

### Page Content (Page 18)
**Category**: Raw Text

> Management styles
> 5. Persuasive
> They share an honest rationale behind decision-making policies that
> can foster an inclusive and trusting environment.
> Pros: It can be especially helpful when leading a less experienced
> team.
> Cons: It’s a one-way communication process and employees don’t
> necessarily have an avenue to give feedback.
> 6. Transformational
> Leaders with this style often push their employees to set and reach
> goals even if it makes them slightly uncomfortable.
> Pros: Adaptability, problem-solving and innovation can be especially
> useful for companies in competitive industries that change quickly.
> Cons: This management style requires the right kind of employees.

---

### Page Content (Page 19)
**Category**: Raw Text

> Management styles
> 7. Transactional
> Relies on attaining goals through structure, supervision and a system of
> rewards and punishments.
>  Pros: This style works well with self-motivated employees and establishes
> unity throughout the company.
>  Cons: This style does not address overall company goals or long-term
> goals and may not motivate all types of employees.
> 8. Collaborative
> Because of their emphasis on employee satisfaction and teamwork, they tend
> to achieve higher levels of respect.
>  These types of leaders are exceptionally skilled in building employee
> morale and helping people re-engage with their work.
>  Pros: They are more in-the-know when it comes to the challenges of
> getting work done on their team.
> Cons: They might find it difficult to create time and space for high-level,
> strategic planning.
