> Cost of Production
> Short Run Costs and Long Run Costs:
> • Short run is a period of time within which the firm can change its
> output by changing only the amount of variable factors, such as labor and
> raw materials etc.
> • In short period, fixed factors such as land, machinery etc, cannot be
> changed. Costs of production incurred in the short run i.e., on variable
> factors are called short run costs.

> Cost of Production
> Fixed and Variable Costs:
> 1. Fixed cost: denoted by (FC) are those that do not vary as a firm
> varies its output. Managers often refer to their fixed costs as
> overhead costs. These are costs that must be incurred in the short run
> even if the firm doesn’t produce anything.
> 2. Variable cost: denoted by (VC) are those that change with output.
> These are the costs of variable inputs.

> Cost of Production
> Average Cost:
> Average cost (AC): is the total cost of producing any
> given output divided by the number of units produced, or
> the cost per unit:
> AC=TC/Q

### Page Content (Page 13)
**Category**: Raw Text

> Cost of Production
> Average cost may be divided two parts:
> 1- Average variable cost (AVC): is variable cost divided by the number
> of units of output produced over a given period. AVC=FC/Q
> 2-Average fixed cost (AFC): is fixed cost divided by the number of
> nits of output produced over a given period
> AFC =FC/Q
> Accordingly, Ac= AVC+ AFC

> Marginal cost is the addition to the total cost as a result of a unit (one
> unit) increase in the output.

> output
