The Passive Voice

In ESP, the passive voice is frequently used because the action or the result is more important than who did it.

Form: Object + be + past participle (+ by Agent)

Active: The engineers tested the system.
Passive: The system was tested (by the engineers).

Conditional Sentences (If-Clauses)

TypeUsageStructureExample
ZeroFacts, general truthsIf + Present Simple, Present SimpleIf you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
FirstReal/possible future situationsIf + Present Simple, will + verbIf the company reduces prices, sales will increase.
SecondUnreal/hypothetical presentIf + Past Simple, would + verbIf I had more time, I would take another course.
ThirdUnreal past (regrets/critiques)If + Past Perfect, would have + past participleIf they had studied harder, they would have passed.

Grammar: Present Perfect Continuous

Form: Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing
Question: Have/Has + subject + been + verb-ing?
Main Uses:

- Action started in the past and continuing into the present.

- Action finished recently with visible results.

⚠️ Note: Stative verbs (know, own, believe) are NOT used in continuous.

Phrases of Purpose

StructureFormExample
to + base verbSimple intentionEngineers installed sensors to monitor temperature.
in order to + base verbFormalThe firm conducted a study in order to reduce risk.
so as to + base verbFormal writtenThe team encrypted data so as to protect information.
so that + clauseDifferent subjectThe company lowered prices so that customers could afford it.
for + noun / -ingFunction/useThis platform is designed for data management.
for the purpose of + -ingVery formalData was collected for the purpose of evaluating performance.
with the aim of + -ingStrategyThe company expanded with the aim of increasing profits.
with a view to + -ingBusiness/diplomaticThe firm entered negotiations with a view to forming a partnership.

Diplomacy Terms

Declare
To state something officially, formally, and publicly.
De Facto
Existing in reality, even if not legally recognised.
De Jure
Existing by law or legally recognised.
Defiance
Open resistance or refusal to obey authority.
Deflect
To redirect attention, criticism, or pressure away from a sensitive issue.
Delegation
A group of authorised representatives sent to negotiate or attend a conference.
Deportment
A diplomat's manner, behaviour, and professional conduct.
Diplomatic Immunity
Legal protection granted to diplomats, exempting them from certain laws of the host country.
Persona Non Grata
Latin term meaning "an unwelcome person" — a diplomat who must leave the host country.
Envoy
An official representative sent on a diplomatic mission.
Ambassador
The highest-ranking diplomatic representative of a country.
Consul
An official who assists citizens abroad and promotes trade relations.
Bilateral Relations
Diplomatic relations between two countries.
Multilateral Diplomacy
Diplomatic negotiations involving three or more states.
Treaty
A formal and legally binding agreement between states.
Sanctions
Penalties imposed by one country or group on another to influence behaviour.
Mediation
The act of assisting two or more parties in reaching a peaceful agreement.
Negotiation
A formal discussion aimed at reaching an agreement.
Protocol
The accepted rules of diplomatic conduct and formal procedure.
Ratify
To formally approve and confirm an agreement or treaty.

However, they frequently face challenges such as lack of funding and dangerous operational environments."

"UN peacekeeping operations are essential tools for transitioning nations from conflict to peace. While they do not replace political solutions, they provide critical security and infrastructural support. Modern peacekeepers perform diverse roles, including protecting civilians, restoring the rule of law, assisting in disarmament, and promoting human rights.
