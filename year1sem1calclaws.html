1. Limits

Basic Limit Laws

Definition:

\[\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = L\]

means for each \(\epsilon > 0\), there exists \(\delta > 0\) such that if \(0 < |x-c| < \delta\), then \(|f(x)-L| < \epsilon\)

Basic Limits:

\[\lim_{x \to c} b = b\] \[\lim_{x \to c} x = c\] \[\lim_{x \to c} x^n = c^n\]

Limit Properties

Property Formula
Scalar Multiple \(\lim_{x \to c} [bf(x)] = b\lim_{x \to c} f(x)\)
Sum/Difference \(\lim_{x \to c} [f(x) \pm g(x)] = \lim_{x \to c} f(x) \pm \lim_{x \to c} g(x)\)
Product \(\lim_{x \to c} [f(x) \cdot g(x)] = \lim_{x \to c} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \to c} g(x)\)
Quotient \(\lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\lim_{x \to c} f(x)}{\lim_{x \to c} g(x)}\), if \(\lim_{x \to c} g(x) \neq 0\)
Power \(\lim_{x \to c} [f(x)]^n = \left[\lim_{x \to c} f(x)\right]^n\)

Important Limit Theorems

Squeeze Theorem:

If \( g(x) \le f(x) \le h(x) \) for all \( x \) in an open interval containing \( c \) (except possibly at \( c \)), and if:

\[\lim_{x \to c} g(x) = L = \lim_{x \to c} h(x)\]

Then: \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = L\)

Cauchy's Theorem:

\[\lim_{x \to a} \frac{x^n - a^n}{x - a} = na^{n-1}\]

General Cauchy:

\[\lim_{x \to a} \frac{x^n - a^n}{x^m - a^m} = \frac{n}{m}a^{n-m}\]

Special Case:

\[\lim_{x \to a} \frac{x - a}{x^m - a^m} = \frac{1}{m}a^{1-m}\]

Trigonometric Limits

\[\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\] \[\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1\] \[\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin ax}{ax} = 1\] \[\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan ax}{ax} = 1\] \[\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1-\cos x}{x^2} = \frac{1}{2}\]

Exponential Limits

The Number e:

\[e = \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{1}{x}\right)^x = \lim_{n \to 0} (1+n)^{\frac{1}{n}}\]

General Exponential Limit:

\[\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{\alpha}{x}\right)^x = e^\alpha\]

2. Continuity

Definition of Continuity at a Point:

A function \(f\) is continuous at \(c\) if:

  1. \(f(c)\) is defined
  2. \(\lim_{x \to c} f(x)\) exists
  3. \(f(c) = \lim_{x \to c} f(x)\)

Alternative Definition:

\[\lim_{h \to 0} [f(c+h) - f(c)] = 0\]

Continuous Functions

The following functions are continuous at every point in their domains:

  • Polynomials
  • Rational functions (\( q(x) \neq 0 \))
  • Radical functions
  • Trigonometric functions
  • Exponential and Logarithmic functions

Properties of Continuity:

If \( f \) and \( g \) are continuous at \( c \), then the following are also continuous at \( c \):

  • Scalar Multiple: \( bf \)
  • Sum/Difference: \( f \pm g \)
  • Product: \( fg \)
  • Quotient: \( f/g \) (if \( g(c) \neq 0 \))
Important: If \(f\) is differentiable at \(c\), then \(f\) is continuous at \(c\).

6.5 Hyperbolic Functions

Identities & Properties:

\[\cosh^2 x - \sinh^2 x = 1, \quad 1 - \tanh^2 x = \text{sech}^2 x\]

Odd/Even: \(\sinh(-x) = -\sinh x\) (Odd), \(\cosh(-x) = \cosh x\) (Even)

Values at zero: \(\sinh(0) = 0, \quad \cosh(0) = 1\)

Limits: \[\lim_{x \to \infty} \sinh x = \infty, \quad \lim_{x \to -\infty} \sinh x = -\infty\] \[\lim_{x \to \infty} \cosh x = \infty, \quad \lim_{x \to -\infty} \cosh x = \infty\]

Asymptotic Behavior: For large \(x\), \(\sinh x \approx \frac{1}{2}e^x\) and \(\cosh x \approx \frac{1}{2}e^x\).

7. Higher Derivatives & Implicit Differentiation

Implicit Differentiation Guidelines:

  1. Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\).
  2. Collect all \(dy/dx\) terms on one side.
  3. Factor out \(dy/dx\) and solve.

Leibniz's Rule (Generalized Product Rule):

\[ \frac{d^n}{dx^n}[f(x)g(x)] = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} f^{(n-k)}(x)g^{(k)}(x) \]

8. Applications & Related Rates

Procedure for Related Rates:

  1. Identify given rates and required rates.
  2. Write equations relating variables.
  3. Differentiate with respect to time (\(t\)).
  4. Substitute known values and solve.
