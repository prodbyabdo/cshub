A computer is a machine that can be programmed to carry out sequences of arithmetic or logical operations (computation) automatically. Modern digital electronic computers can perform generic sets of operations known as programs. These programs enable computers to perform a wide range of tasks.

The study of computers and computational systems is known as Computer Science. Unlike electrical and computer engineers, computer scientists deal mostly with software and software systems; this includes their theory, design, development, and application.

Hardware & Architecture

Computer hardware includes the physical parts of a computer, such as the case, central processing unit (CPU), monitor, mouse, keyboard, computer data storage, graphics card, sound card, speakers and motherboard. The design and structure of these components is studied under Computer Architecture.

Multiple computers can be connected together to form Networks. The Internet is the most prominent example, allowing global communication and data exchange through protocols like TCP/IP.

Software & Systems

Software refers to parts of the computer which do not have a material form, such as programs, data, protocols, etc. The discipline of designing, writing, testing, and maintaining software is known as Software Engineering.

Programming

Programming is the process of creating a set of instructions that tell a computer how to perform a task. It can be done using a variety of computer programming languages, such as JavaScript, Python, and C++. Modern Web Development heavily relies on these languages to create interactive websites and applications.

Operating Systems

An Operating System (OS) is system software that manages computer hardware, software resources, and provides common services for computer programs. Time-sharing operating systems schedule tasks for efficient use of the system and may also include accounting software for cost allocation of processor time, mass storage, printing, and other resources.

Mathematics & Theory

The theoretical foundations of computation are heavily rooted in Discrete Mathematics, encompassing logic, set theory, graph theory, and combinatorics.

At the core of software design are Algorithms (step-by-step procedures for calculations) and Data Structures (ways to organize and store data efficiently for access and modification).

Data & Intelligence

Information systems rely on Databases to store, retrieve, and manage massive amounts of structured and unstructured data reliably.

More recently, Artificial Intelligence (AI) has emerged as a major field, enabling computers to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and translation between languages. Protecting these systems and their data is the domain of Cybersecurity.
