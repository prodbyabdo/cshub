MULTIPLE & TRIPLE INTEGRALS PRACTICE - LECTURE 8

Multiple & Triple Integrals Practice

Problem 1
\[ \int_0^1\int_0^x xy\,dy\,dx \]
Inner: \(\int_0^x xy\,dy = \frac{xy^2}{2}\Big|_0^x=\frac{x^3}{2}\)
Outer: \(\int_0^1\frac{x^3}{2}dx=\frac{1}{8}\)
\[ = \frac{1}{8} \]
Problem 2
\[ \int_0^{\pi}\int_0^{\sin x} dy\,dx \]
Inner: \(\sin x\)
Outer: \(\int_0^\pi \sin x\,dx = 2\)
\[ = 2 \]
Problem 3
\[ \int\int_R (x+y)\,dA,\; R=[0,1]\times[0,2] \]
\(\int_0^1\int_0^2(x+y)dy\,dx\)
Inner: \([xy+\frac{y^2}{2}]_0^2 = 2x+2\)
Outer: \([x^2+2x]_0^1 = 3\)
\[ = 3 \]
Problem 4
\[ \int_0^1\int_0^1\int_0^1 (x+y+z)\,dx\,dy\,dz \]
By symmetry each variable contributes \(\frac{1}{2}\)
Total: \(3\times\frac{1}{2}=\frac{3}{2}\)
\[ = \frac{3}{2} \]
Problem 5
\[ \text{Volume under }z=1-x^2-y^2\text{ above }z=0 \]
Region: \(x^2+y^2\le 1\); use polar
\(\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1(1-r^2)r\,dr\,d\theta = 2\pi\cdot\frac{1}{4}\)
\[ = \frac{\pi}{2} \]
