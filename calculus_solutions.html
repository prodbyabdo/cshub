1. Basic Limits

Problem 1
\[ \lim_{x \to 2} (4x^2 + 3) \]
Apply limit laws (limit of sum is sum of limits, limit of constant is constant):
\[ = 4 \lim_{x \to 2} x^2 + \lim_{x \to 2} 3 \]
Substitute \( x = 2 \):
\[ = 4(2)^2 + 3 = 4(4) + 3 = 16 + 3 \]
\[ = 19 \]
Problem 2
\[ \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x-2}{x+2} \]
Direct substitution:
\[ = \frac{3-2}{3+2} \]
\[ = \frac{1}{5} \]
Problem 3
\[ \lim_{x \to 4} \sqrt{25-x^2} \]
Using limit laws for radicals:
\[ = \sqrt{\lim_{x \to 4}(25-x^2)} \]
Substitute \( x = 4 \):
\[ = \sqrt{25 - 4^2} = \sqrt{25 - 16} = \sqrt{9} \]
\[ = 3 \]
Problem 4
\[ \lim_{x \to 1} (3x^2-1)(9x+2) \]
Direct substitution:
\[ = (3(1)^2 - 1)(9(1) + 2) \]
\[ = (3-1)(9+2) = (2)(11) \]
\[ = 22 \]

\[ = 4 \]

2. Limits at Infinity & Indeterminate Forms

Problem 5
\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3+x}{2+x} \]
Divide numerator and denominator by highest power of x (which is x):
\[ = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3/x + 1}{2/x + 1} \]
As \( x \to \infty \), \( 1/x \to 0 \).
\[ = \frac{0 + 1}{0 + 1} \]
\[ = 1 \]
Problem 6
\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{4x+1}{x+9} \]
Divide by x:
\[ = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{4 + 1/x}{1 + 9/x} = \frac{4+0}{1+0} \]
\[ = 4 \]
Problem 7
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{x^2-1}{x+1} \]
Direct substitution gives \( 0/0 \). Factor the numerator:
\[ x^2 - 1 = (x-1)(x+1) \]
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x+1} = \lim_{x \to -1} (x-1) \]
Substitute \( x = -1 \):
\[ = -2 \]
Problem 8
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{x^2+3x+2}{x+1} \]
Direct sub: \( 1-3+2 = 0 \), denom 0. Factor numerator:
\[ x^2+3x+2 = (x+1)(x+2) \]
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{(x+1)(x+2)}{x+1} = \lim_{x \to -1} (x+2) \]
\[ = 1 \]
Problem 9
\[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^5-32}{x^2-4} \]
0/0 Indeterminate form. Use L'Hopital's Rule or factoring.
Using L'Hopital: \( \frac{d}{dx}(x^5-32) = 5x^4 \), \( \frac{d}{dx}(x^2-4) = 2x \).
\[ \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{5x^4}{2x} = \frac{5(16)}{2(2)} = \frac{80}{4} \]
\[ = 20 \]
Problem 10
\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x+\sin x}{\tan x + x} \]
Divide numerator and denominator by x:
\[ = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 + \frac{\sin x}{x}}{\frac{\tan x}{x} + 1} \]
We know \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \) and \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan x}{x} = 1 \).
\[ = \frac{1+1}{1+1} = \frac{2}{2} \]
\[ = 1 \]

\[ \lim_{x \to 5} \frac{x-5}{\sqrt{x-3}-\sqrt{2}} \]
Multiply by conjugate \( \frac{\sqrt{x-3}+\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{x-3}+\sqrt{2}} \):
Num: \( (x-5)(\sqrt{x-3}+\sqrt{2}) \)
Denom: \( (x-3) - 2 = x-5 \)
Cancel \( x-5 \):
\[ \lim_{x \to 5} (\sqrt{x-3}+\sqrt{2}) = \sqrt{5-3} + \sqrt{2} = \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2} \]
\[ = 2\sqrt{2} \]

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{x(2+x)}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} (2+x) \]
\[ = 2 \]

\[ \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^6-1}{x^2-1} \]
Factor \( x^6-1 \) as difference of squares \( (x^3-1)(x^3+1) \), and \( x^2-1 \) as \( (x-1)(x+1) \).
Better yet, L'Hopital's rule:
Num deriv: \( 6x^5 \), Denom deriv: \( 2x \).

\[ = 3 \]

Exercise 1.3.1 (Continuity Properties)

Ex 1.3.1 - 1
Prove that \( f(x) = \sin(ax) \), \( a \ne 0 \), is continuous on \( \mathbb{R} \).
The function \( g(x) = ax \) is a polynomial (linear), so it is continuous everywhere.
The function \( h(u) = \sin(u) \) is a standard trigonometric function, continuous everywhere.
The composition of continuous functions is continuous. Thus \( f(x) = h(g(x)) \) is continuous on \( \mathbb{R} \).
\[ \text{Proven} \]
Ex 1.3.1 - 2
Prove that \( f(x) = x^3 - x \) is continuous on \( \mathbb{R} \).
\( f(x) \) is a polynomial function.
All polynomial functions are continuous on their entire domain \( \mathbb{R} \).
\[ \text{Proven} \]
Ex 1.3.1 - 3
Discuss the continuity of \( f(x) = \ln(x^2+1) \).
\( g(x) = x^2+1 \) is continuous everywhere and \( x^2+1 \ge 1 > 0 \).
\( \ln(u) \) is continuous for \( u > 0 \).
Since the inner function is always positive and continuous, the composition is continuous everywhere.
\[ \text{Continuous on } \mathbb{R} \]

First Principles & Advanced Derivatives

Ex 2.4.2 (i)
First principles: \( y = x^3 \)
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)^3 - x^3}{h} \)
\( = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{x^3 + 3x^2h + 3xh^2 + h^3 - x^3}{h} \)
\( = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{h(3x^2 + 3xh + h^2)}{h} \)
\( = 3x^2 + 0 + 0 \)
\[ y' = 3x^2 \]
Ex 2.4.2 (ii)
First principles: \( y = \frac{x}{x^2-5} \)
\( \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{h} \left[ \frac{x+h}{(x+h)^2-5} - \frac{x}{x^2-5} \right] \)
Common denominator: \( ((x+h)^2-5)(x^2-5) \).
Num: \( (x+h)(x^2-5) - x((x+h)^2-5) \)
\( = x^3-5x+hx^2-5h - (x(x^2+2xh+h^2)-5x) \)
\( = x^3-5x+hx^2-5h - x^3-2x^2h-xh^2+5x \)
\( = -x^2h - xh^2 - 5h = h(-x^2-xh-5) \).
Divide by h: \( -x^2-xh-5 \). Let \( h \to 0 \).
\[ y' = \frac{-x^2-5}{(x^2-5)^2} \]
Ex 2.4.2 (iii)
First principles: \( y = \sin^2 x \)
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sin^2(x+h) - \sin^2 x}{h} \).
Use difference of squares: \( (\sin(x+h)-\sin x)(\sin(x+h)+\sin x) \).
\( \sin(x+h)-\sin x = 2\sin(h/2)\cos(x+h/2) \).
Limit: \( \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{2\sin(h/2)}{h} \cdot \cos(x+h/2) \cdot (\sin(x+h)+\sin x) \).
\( 1 \cdot \cos x \cdot (2\sin x) = 2\sin x \cos x \).
\[ y' = \sin(2x) \]
Ex 2.4.2 (iv)
First principles: \( y = x e^{-x} \)
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)e^{-(x+h)} - xe^{-x}}{h} \).
Factor \( e^{-x} \): \( e^{-x} \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)e^{-h} - x}{h} \).
\( e^{-x} \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{xe^{-h} + he^{-h} - x}{h} = e^{-x} \lim_{h \to 0} ( x\frac{e^{-h}-1}{h} + e^{-h} ) \).
Recalling \( \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{e^{-h}-1}{h} = -1 \).
\( e^{-x} (x(-1) + 1) \).
\[ y' = e^{-x}(1-x) \]
Prob 2(a)
\[ y = \sin(x\cos(2x)) \]

\[ y' = [\cos(2x) - 2x\sin(2x)]\cos(x\cos(2x)) \]
Prob 2(b)

Quotient rule. \( u=x^2+ax+b, u'=2x+a \). \( v=x^2-ax+b, v'=2x-a \).
\( Num = (2x+a)(x^2-ax+b) - (x^2+ax+b)(2x-a) \).

Cancel \( 2x^3 \), \( 2bx \), \( a^2x \). Terms remaining: \( -ax^2 - ax^2 + ab + ab = -2ax^2 + 2ab \).

Inner 1: \( 0 + \frac{1}{2}(2x-1)^{-1/2} \cdot 2 = (2x-1)^{-1/2} \).
\[ y' = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{1+\sqrt{2x-1}} \sqrt{2x-1}} \]

Chain: \( 2\sin(\dots)\cos(\dots) \cdot \text{Inner}' \).

Recall \( 2\sin A \cos A = \sin(2A) \).
\[ y' = \frac{2}{(x+1)^2} \sin\left(\frac{4x}{x+1}\right) \]

\[ y = \frac{1+2\tan^2 x}{1-\tan^2 x} \]
Simplify? \( \frac{1+\tan^2 x + \tan^2 x}{1-\tan^2 x} = \frac{\sec^2 x + \tan^2 x}{1-\tan^2 x} \). Maybe just Quotient.

4. Inverse Trigonometric Differentiation

Problem 2
\[ y = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{a}\right) + 2\sin^{-1}\left(\frac{a}{x}\right) \]
1. \( \frac{d}{dx}\tan^{-1} u = \frac{u'}{1+u^2} \). \( u=\frac{x}{a}, u'=\frac{1}{a} \).
\[ \frac{1/a}{1+(x/a)^2} = \frac{1/a}{1+x^2/a^2} = \frac{1/a}{(a^2+x^2)/a^2} = \frac{a}{a^2+x^2} \]
2. \( \frac{d}{dx}\sin^{-1} u = \frac{u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}} \). \( u=ax^{-1}, u'=-ax^{-2} \).
\[ 2 \cdot \frac{-a/x^2}{\sqrt{1-(a/x)^2}} = \frac{-2a/x^2}{\sqrt{(x^2-a^2)/x^2}} = \frac{-2a/x^2}{|1/x|\sqrt{x^2-a^2}} \]
Assuming \( x>0 \), \( \frac{-2a}{x\sqrt{x^2-a^2}} \).
\[ y' = \frac{a}{a^2+x^2} - \frac{2a}{x\sqrt{x^2-a^2}} \]
Problem 3
\[ y = \frac{\cos^{-1}(x^2)}{x+1} \]
Quotient Rule: \( u = \cos^{-1}(x^2), u' = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-(x^2)^2}}(2x) = \frac{-2x}{\sqrt{1-x^4}} \).
\( v = x+1, v' = 1 \).
\[ y' = \frac{\frac{-2x(x+1)}{\sqrt{1-x^4}} - \cos^{-1}(x^2)}{(x+1)^2} \]
Problem 4
\[ y = e^{x^2}\sin^{-1}x \]
Product Rule: \( (2x e^{x^2})(\sin^{-1}x) + (e^{x^2})(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}) \).
\[ y' = e^{x^2} \left[ 2x\sin^{-1}x + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \right] \]
Problem 5
\[ y = \sin^{-1}(2x) + \cos^{-1}(2x) \]
Identity: \( \sin^{-1}u + \cos^{-1}u = \frac{\pi}{2} \) (constant).
Derivative of a constant is 0.
\[ y' = 0 \]
Problem 6
\[ y = x\cos^{-1}x - \sqrt{1-x^2} \]
Part 1: \( 1\cdot\cos^{-1}x + x(\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}) \).
Part 2: \( -\frac{1}{2}(1-x^2)^{-1/2}(-2x) = \frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \).
Sum: \( \cos^{-1}x - \frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} + \frac{x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \). Terms cancel.
\[ y' = \cos^{-1}x \]
Problem 7
\[ y = \frac{1}{2}\left[ x\sqrt{4-x^2} + 4\sin^{-1}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) \right] \]
Deriv of \( x\sqrt{4-x^2} \): \( 1\cdot\sqrt{4-x^2} + x\frac{-2x}{2\sqrt{4-x^2}} = \frac{4-x^2-x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} = \frac{4-2x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} \).
Deriv of \( 4\sin^{-1}(x/2) \): \( 4 \frac{1/2}{\sqrt{1-(x/2)^2}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{(4-x^2)/4}} = \frac{4}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} \).
Total: \( \frac{1}{2} [ \frac{4-2x^2+4}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} ] = \frac{1}{2} \frac{8-2x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} = \frac{4-x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} \).
\[ y' = \sqrt{4-x^2} \]
Problem 8
\[ y = \cos x \cdot \sec^{-1}x \]
Product rule: \( -\sin x \sec^{-1}x + \cos x \frac{1}{|x|\sqrt{x^2-1}} \).
\[ y' = \frac{\cos x}{|x|\sqrt{x^2-1}} - \sin x \sec^{-1}x \]
Problem 9
\[ y = e^{\sin^{-1}x} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^2}} \]
Term 1: \( e^{\sin^{-1}x} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} \).
Term 2: \( (1+x^2)^{-1/2} \to -\frac{1}{2}(1+x^2)^{-3/2}(2x) = -x(1+x^2)^{-3/2} \).
\[ y' = \frac{e^{\sin^{-1}x}}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} - \frac{x}{(1+x^2)^{3/2}} \]
Problem 10
\[ y = \csc\left[\frac{x}{x+1}\right] - \sec^{-1}\left(\frac{x+1}{x}\right) \]
Note if we assume \( \sec^{-1}(\frac{x+1}{x}) = \cos^{-1}(\frac{x}{x+1}) \).
Let \( u = \frac{x}{x+1} \). \( y = \csc u - \cos^{-1} u \).
\( u' = \frac{1(x+1)-x(1)}{(x+1)^2} = \frac{1}{(x+1)^2} \).
\( y' = -\csc u \cot u \cdot u' - (\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1-u^2}} \cdot u') \).
\[ y' = \frac{1}{(x+1)^2} \left[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(\frac{x}{x+1})^2}} - \csc\left(\frac{x}{x+1}\right)\cot\left(\frac{x}{x+1}\right) \right] \]
Problem 11
\[ y = \frac{\cot^{-1}x}{1+x^2} \]
Quotient rule. \( u = \cot^{-1}x, u' = \frac{-1}{1+x^2} \). \( v = 1+x^2, v'=2x \).
\( Num = \frac{-1}{1+x^2}(1+x^2) - \cot^{-1}x(2x) = -1 - 2x\cot^{-1}x \).
\[ y' = -\frac{1+2x\cot^{-1}x}{(1+x^2)^2} \]
Problem 12
\[ y = \ln(x - \tan x) \]
Chain rule: \( \frac{1}{x-\tan x} (1-\sec^2 x) \).
Recall \( 1-\sec^2 x = -\tan^2 x \).
\[ y' = \frac{-\tan^2 x}{x-\tan x} \]
Problem 13
\[ y = \csc^{-1}\left(\frac{1-x}{x+1}\right) \]
Let \( u = \frac{1-x}{x+1} \). \( u' = \frac{-1(x+1)-(1-x)(1)}{(x+1)^2} = \frac{-2}{(x+1)^2} \).
\( \frac{d}{dx}\csc^{-1} u = \frac{-u'}{|u|\sqrt{u^2-1}} \).
This derivative is complex to simplify fully inline, but plugging in parts:
\[ y' = \frac{2}{(x+1)^2 |\frac{1-x}{x+1}| \sqrt{(\frac{1-x}{x+1})^2-1}} \]
Problem 14
\[ y = \ln\sqrt{\frac{x+1}{x-1}} + \tan^{-1}(2x) \]
Simplify Log: \( \frac{1}{2}(\ln(x+1) - \ln(x-1)) \).
Deriv Log part: \( \frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{x+1} - \frac{1}{x-1}) = \frac{1}{2}\frac{x-1-(x+1)}{x^2-1} = \frac{-1}{x^2-1} \).
Deriv tan: \( \frac{2}{1+(2x)^2} = \frac{2}{1+4x^2} \).
