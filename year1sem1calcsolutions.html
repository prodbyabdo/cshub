2. Limits at Infinity & Indeterminate Forms

Problem 5
\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3+x}{2+x} \]
Divide numerator and denominator by highest power of x (which is x):
\[ = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3/x + 1}{2/x + 1} \]
As \( x \to \infty \), \( 1/x \to 0 \).
\[ = \frac{0 + 1}{0 + 1} \]
\[ = 1 \]

Divide by x:

\[ = 4 \]
Problem 7
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{x^2-1}{x+1} \]
Direct substitution gives \( 0/0 \). Factor the numerator:
\[ x^2 - 1 = (x-1)(x+1) \]
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x+1} = \lim_{x \to -1} (x-1) \]
Substitute \( x = -1 \):
\[ = -2 \]

\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{x^2+3x+2}{x+1} \]
Direct sub: \( 1-3+2 = 0 \), denom 0. Factor numerator:
\[ x^2+3x+2 = (x+1)(x+2) \]
\[ \lim_{x \to -1} \frac{(x+1)(x+2)}{x+1} = \lim_{x \to -1} (x+2) \]
\[ = 1 \]

Assuming \( x>0 \), \( \frac{-2a}{x\sqrt{x^2-a^2}} \).

Example 1.3.3
Evaluate \( \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2+x-2}{x-1} \).
Direct substitution gives \( 0/0 \).
Factor the numerator: \( x^2+x-2 = (x-1)(x+2) \).
\( \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{(x-1)(x+2)}{x-1} = \lim_{x \to 1} (x+2) = 1+2 = 3 \).
\[ = 3 \]

Result: \( 1 \cdot \frac{1}{\cos 0} = 1 \cdot 1 = 1 \).
\[ = 1 \]

Lecture 3: Continuity & First Principles

Example 1.2.1
Prove that \( f(x) = x^2 \) is continuous on \( \mathbb{R} \).
Choose an arbitrary point \( x=c \in \mathbb{R} \). We must show \( \lim_{h \to 0} [f(c+h) - f(c)] = 0 \).
\( \lim_{h \to 0} [(c+h)^2 - c^2] = \lim_{h \to 0} [c^2 + 2ch + h^2 - c^2] \).
\( = \lim_{h \to 0} [h(2c+h)] = 0 \cdot (2c+0) = 0 \).
\[ f(x) \text{ is continuous at } c \implies \text{continuous on } \mathbb{R}. \]
Example 1.2.3
Prove that \( f(x) = a^x \) (\( a>0 \)) is continuous on \( \mathbb{R} \).
Choose arbitrary \( x=c \in \mathbb{R} \).
\( \lim_{h \to 0} [f(c+h) - f(c)] = \lim_{h \to 0} [a^{c+h} - a^c] \).
\( = \lim_{h \to 0} a^c(a^h - 1) = a^c(a^0 - 1) = a^c(1-1) = 0 \).
\[ \text{Continuous on } \mathbb{R}. \]
Example 2.1.8
If \( y = x^3 \), find the rate of change \( \frac{\Delta y}{h} \).
\( \Delta y = (x+h)^3 - x^3 \).
Expand: \( x^3 + 3x^2h + 3xh^2 + h^3 - x^3 = 3x^2h + 3xh^2 + h^3 \).
\( \frac{\Delta y}{h} = \frac{h(3x^2 + 3xh + h^2)}{h} \).
\[ = 3x^2 + 3xh + h^2 \]
Example 2.3.14
Find the derivative of \( y = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \) from first principles.
\( f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\frac{1}{\sqrt{x+h}} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}}{h} \).
